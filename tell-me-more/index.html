Philly 250

The nation's 250th anniversary celebration is upon us, and here at XPN we're commemorating in four ways. At the center of all of them, we want your input.

We're asking you to submit two categories of things:

  • Pivotal Philly music moments — like David Bowie recording "Young Americans" at Sigma Sound Studios, the inaugural Roots Picnic in 2008, or Sister Rosetta Tharpe's legendary 1957 arrival at 30th Street Station.
  • Songs for, by, and about Philadelphia — like Bruce Springsteen's "Streets of Philadelphia," or songs by G Love, Hall & Oates, Patti LaBelle, Japanese Breakfast, Ween, Joan Jett, Elton John, or Dr. Dog that are tied to this city.

Drop your submissions for consideration using the form here:

Submit Your Nominations — Deadline March 19

We'll compile a comprehensive list of submissions and whittle it down to a curated 250-song playlist running 4th of July weekend. Additionally, we'll release a list of more than 40 essential moments in Philly music history.

This is just one of four Philly 250 projects we're working on. Here's what's coming:

  • Philly Anthems — An original music project with Eric Bazilian (The Hooters), Eliza Hardy Jones (The War On Drugs), Devon Gilfillian, and Christian McBride. Supported by The Pew Center for Arts & Heritage.
  • World Cafe: Sense of Place — A deep dive into Philadelphia's musical DNA.
  • Sound of History — A Juneteenth performance by Zeek Burse and Laurin Talese.
WXPN Artist to Watch
Ratboys
Chicago, IL

Chicago indie rock band Ratboys have spent a decade quietly becoming one of the most emotionally precise bands working today. Vocalist and rhythm guitarist Julia Steiner and lead guitarist Dave Sagan met as Notre Dame freshmen and have since built a catalog of heartfelt, Midwestern-tinged rock that keeps finding new emotional territory.

"Not only do Chicago stalwarts Ratboys continue to fine-tune their approach to the genre with each record, they also sound increasingly inspired."

— Pitchfork

Their sixth album Singin' to an Empty Chair, produced by Chris Walla at Chicago's Electrical Audio and Rosebud Studios, is 11 tracks of introspective, unhurried indie rock — "about as good as American indie rock gets," per Stereogum.

Steiner and Sagan built Ratboys slowly, releasing records that feel considered rather than rushed. The new album sits with the weight of contemporary life without flinching. "One of the most exciting bands working right now," says Aquarium Drunkard.

Essential tracks: "The Window," "It's Alive," "Hetty Green," "My Drag," "I Go Out at Night"
